The Story

Zach

27 years old — Founder & Head Curator

Z ach didn't set out to open a liquor store. He set out to understand one. At 22, he left a desk job and walked into the first craft cocktail bar he could find and asked if he could sweep floors. They handed him a mop. Six months later, he was behind the bar.

Over the next four years, he worked in some of Indianapolis and Louisville's best bars — places where the back bar was treated like a museum and every pour came with a conversation. He earned his WSET Level 2 in Spirits, completed a mixology intensive in Chicago, and spent a summer traveling through Kentucky distilleries on a self-funded research trip he refers to simply as "the education."

But the real education had started decades earlier, in his grandmother's living room. Gloria would pour two fingers of something special — Macallan or Hibiki or whatever she'd been gifted that year — and she'd hold it up to the light before she'd even smell it. "You have to look at it first," she'd say. "It already has something to tell you."

When Gloria passed in 2019, she left Zach a small cabinet of bottles and a handwritten list of the ones she'd always wanted to try. He opened Gloria's in 2026 to finish that list — and to help others start their own.

The Craft

Behind Every Bottle Is a Decision.

Zach selects every bottle on the shelf personally.

Most liquor stores fill their shelves based on distributor relationships and margin. Zach fills his based on a simple question his grandmother used to ask: "Will this make them feel something?"

He tastes everything before it earns a place on the shelf. He travels to meet producers. He reads the distillery notes and then sets them aside — because what he really wants to know is what the bottle says to him, alone, in a quiet room.

The result is a selection that's genuinely curated, not just assembled. Every spirit at Gloria's was chosen because it moved somebody — and Zach believes it will move you too.
